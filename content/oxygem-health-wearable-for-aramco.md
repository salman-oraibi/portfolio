Most health wearables solve the data problem and ignore the behaviour problem. They measure faithfully but rarely change anything. When Aramco commissioned the development of OXYGEM, the brief was clear: build a health wearable that drives genuine habit modification, not just passive monitoring. I led the project from the first feasibility sketch through to a fully engineered product with a functional mobile application.

## The Problem

Occupational health in high-intensity industrial environments is a persistent challenge. Workers may go through years of routine health screening without ever developing the daily habits that prevent the conditions being screened for. The gap is in engagement — connecting individuals to their own health data in a way that is personally meaningful and motivating, not clinical and forgettable.

The challenge for OXYGEM was to bridge that gap through hardware and software design. The wearable needed to capture relevant health signals in a compact, wearable form factor. The application needed to make that data compelling through gamification — rewarding healthy behaviour, building streaks, and creating a loop of awareness and action.

## What I Built

I managed the project across the full product development cycle. Starting from product definition and concept feasibility, I scoped the technical requirements, validated the sensor configuration, and established the form factor constraints for the device.

The detailed design engineering phase covered industrial design, electronics architecture, and enclosure engineering — developing a device compact enough for daily wear while housing the sensor suite and wireless communication hardware needed to feed the app in real time.

In parallel, I directed the development of the mobile application built around a gamification framework. The app translated biometric data into visible progress metrics, challenges, and reward structures designed to reinforce healthy daily habits. The gamification logic drew on behavioural science principles: short feedback loops, visible streaks, and goal-setting tied to personally relevant health outcomes.

The design was taken through to manufacturing-ready documentation — detailed enough that Aramco could move directly from the prototype phase into production planning.

## The Outcome or Impact

OXYGEM delivered a complete, validated product concept: a health wearable with a functional app, manufacturing-ready design files, and a gamification model validated through user feedback. The project demonstrated that a product could be simultaneously medically meaningful and behaviourally engaging — closing the gap between data collection and habit change.

For Archetype Foundry, OXYGEM became a reference engagement for health tech product development: full-cycle, hardware-software integrated, and grounded in the end user's reality rather than the technology's capabilities.
