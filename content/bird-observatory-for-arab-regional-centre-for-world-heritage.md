Conservation infrastructure is rarely glamorous, but done well it enables the science that everything else depends on. Bahrain's position on the East Atlantic/West Asian flyway makes it a significant staging ground for migratory birds — but that ecological significance only generates conservation value if it can be observed, documented, and communicated. The Arab Regional Centre for World Heritage commissioned Archetype Foundry to design and build a bird observatory that would anchor national conservation efforts. I led the project from concept through physical prototype.

## The Problem

Existing wildlife observation infrastructure in Bahrain was inadequate for systematic migratory bird monitoring. The ARC-WH needed a purpose-designed observatory — not just a shelter with a view, but a structure designed around the behavioural requirements of observation: sightlines, acoustic management, positioning relative to bird movement corridors, and the practical needs of researchers and public visitors using the space over extended periods.

The design also had to respond to Bahrain's climate and coastal environment. The structure would be exposed to salt air, high temperatures, and intense direct sun — constraints that drive material selection and structural design in ways that a conventional building brief rarely makes explicit.

The project required design and build capability, research collaboration with ornithological experts, and a physical prototype that could validate the concept before any permanent installation.

## What I Built

I led a multi-disciplinary team through the full project arc: concept design, research partnership, engineering development, and physical prototype fabrication.

The concept design phase involved site analysis and research collaboration with conservation specialists to establish the observatory's optimal positioning, orientation, and internal configuration. Sightline modelling informed the structural geometry; acoustic analysis shaped the cladding and aperture design to minimise disturbance to birds at close range.

The structural and material engineering resolved the coastal environment constraints: the material palette and connection details were specified for durability in salt air and high UV exposure, without the weight and maintenance overhead of conventional construction approaches.

I managed the fabrication of the physical prototype using digital fabrication and conventional construction methods — a full-scale working model that demonstrated the concept's viability and gave the ARC-WH a concrete artefact for stakeholder engagement, site assessment, and public communication.

## The Outcome or Impact

The bird observatory prototype was delivered to the Arab Regional Centre for World Heritage, contributing to national conservation infrastructure for migratory bird monitoring in Bahrain. The project demonstrated that ecological conservation and design rigour are not competing priorities — a well-designed observatory is a better scientific instrument as well as a more compelling public asset.

Leading a team through a commission that combined architectural design, ecological research, and physical fabrication reinforced a consistent lesson: the best outcomes happen when the people doing the building understand the science behind why it needs to exist.
